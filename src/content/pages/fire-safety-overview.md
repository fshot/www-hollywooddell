The Hollywood Dell Civic Association Fire Safety Committee works to protect our neighborhood from wildfire risk through education, home assessments, and community-wide preparedness.

**Hollywood Dell is a certified [Firewise USA](https://www.nfpa.org/education-and-research/wildfire/firewise-usa) community** — a national designation recognizing our commitment to wildfire preparedness. Maintaining certification requires annual documentation of community fire-safety activities.

## Free Home Risk Assessments

Professional assessors provide **free** property evaluations identifying vulnerabilities and recommending action plans:

### MySafe:LA / Wildfire Alliance
Free home assessments plus monthly virtual townhalls with the latest wildfire news.
- [Request an assessment](https://wildfirela.org/wildfire-home-assessment)
- [Monthly townhall registration](https://wildfirela.org)

### Resource Conservation District (RCDSMM)
Free assessments **plus** potential reimbursement for home hardening improvements.
- Contact Molly Ross: (818) 514-4583
- Website: [rcdsmm.org](https://rcdsmm.org)

### Self-Assessment Tools
- [Faura](https://faura.com) — home risk scoring
- [Rhino](https://www.rhinofire.com) — property assessment
- [NFPA Firewise online training](https://www.nfpa.org/education-and-research/wildfire/firewise-usa) — free courses

## What You Can Do Right Now

1. **Screen your vents** — Install 1/8" steel mesh vent screening. The City of LA previously offered free installation funding (program ended March 2026).
2. **Clear Zone Zero** — Remove all combustible vegetation and materials within 0–5 feet of your home.
3. **Inspect your roof** — Clean gutters and verify you have Class A fire-rated roofing materials.

[Learn more about Home Hardening →](/fire-safety/home-hardening)
