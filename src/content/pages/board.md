## President

![Alexa Iles (Skarpelos)](/images/board-alexa.jpg)

**Alexa Iles (Skarpelos)**

Born and raised in the Pacific Northwest, Alexa moved to San Francisco in 1991 and eventually found her way to the sunny hills of the Hollywood Dell in 2000 when she and her husband, expecting their daughter Athena, settled on La Punta Drive. After 7 years and countless walks up and down the Dell, Alexa knew a lot of her neighbors. But after adopting a rescue dog in late 2007, Alexa got to know the true heart of the Hollywood Dell during her walks through the neighborhood. In 2011 Alexa co-chaired a successful grassroots community effort to preserve a beloved walking trail in the heart of the Hollywood Dell. Today she serves as the HDCA president, serves as Civilian Co-Chair on the Hollywood Community-Police Advisory Board, and on the Hollywood Bowl Community Advisory Group.

## Vice President

![David Brewer](/images/board-david.jpg)

**David Brewer**

David was born and raised on a horse farm in Lexington, Kentucky. He first visited California on a trip to Disneyland in February 1971 during the San Fernando earthquake and fell in love with California. A few years later he moved to Los Angeles. In 1980 David moved into the Dell where he lives with Husband Jim and their cat Samantha.

## Treasurer

![Kelly Ziegler](/images/board-kelly.jpg)

**Kelly Ziegler**

Kelly has lived in Los Angeles most of his life and moved to the Dell in 1999. He graduated from the University of Puget Sound and holds an MBA from Pepperdine University. Kelly works in Finance for Nestlé in Glendale and shares his home with husband Bill and two children. He actively supports his children's school and various nonprofits.

## Secretary

![Jan Hohenstein](/images/board-jan.jpg)

**Jan Hohenstein**

Jan grew up in the southern Appalachian Mountains and eventually reached the Hollywood Dell after living in Florida, Boston, and Atlanta. She has worked as a real estate agent for twenty years and is a Mount Saint Mary's University graduate. She appreciates the community's tight-knit neighborhood feel.
