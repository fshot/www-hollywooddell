*Last amended: March 11, 2020*

The Hollywood Dell Civic Association (HDCA) is a nonprofit, volunteer-run neighborhood association serving nearly 1,100 residences. The area spans from Franklin south to the Hollywood Reservoir, between Cahuenga and Argyle, encompassing 46 named streets.

**Mission:** Support projects benefiting the neighborhood while adhering to 501(c)(3) tax-exempt purposes.

## Article I: Membership

Membership includes anyone living, working, or interested in Hollywood Dell. Annual dues are $50 per household, with discounted rates of $25 for seniors and students.

## Article II: Officers & Positions

The Executive Board consists of President, Vice President, Secretary, and Treasurer, elected annually at February meetings with terms beginning March 1st.

- **President:** Presides over meetings, represents the membership, appoints committees, serves as official spokesperson.
- **Vice President:** Assumes presidential duties when needed; supervises committees, gathers status reports, ensures compliance with bylaws.
- **Secretary:** Maintains official records including meeting minutes, sign-in sheets, and membership documentation; compiles annual reports.
- **Treasurer:** Manages financial records, processes deposits and checks, provides financial reports.

Additional positions: HDCA representatives to external organizations (HUNC, Hillside Federation, Hollywood Bowl), Outreach liaison, Historian, Webmaster, and Committee Chairs.

## Article III: Committees

The President appoints committees as needed. The Vice President serves as Executive Board liaison. Each committee elects its own chair, who must attend regular membership meetings.

Current committees include: Beautification, Building & Development, Crime Prevention & Neighborhood Safety, Dell Events, 501(c)(3) Operations, Franklin/Ivar Park, Friends of La Rocha Trail, Green Team, Outreach, and Traffic/Roads/Parking.

## Article IV: Meetings

Regular membership meetings occur the second Wednesday monthly, open to the public. Voting members present constitute a quorum; Executive Board meetings occur quarterly requiring three members minimum.

## Article V: Elections & Nominations

Nominations open 30 days before February elections; current members may submit candidates. The Board fills vacancies through appointment.

## Article VI: Fiscal Responsibility

Two Executive Board members must approve expenditures; those exceeding $1,000 require membership vote with advance notice. Annual audits by appointed voting members are mandatory.

## Article VII: Bylaw Amendments

Bylaws may be amended by a 60% majority vote of attending voting members, with proposed changes distributed 15 working days in advance.

## Article VIII: General Provisions

Invalid bylaw sections don't affect remaining provisions. No officer holds personal financial liability.
